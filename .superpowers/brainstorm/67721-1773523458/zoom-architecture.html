Two Approaches

A

Unified Three.js Scene (Single Canvas)

One continuous Three.js scene with camera zoom controlling what's visible. Earth at close range, satellites at medium, solar system and probes when fully zoomed out. Seamless transitions via camera animation.

Pros

  • Truly seamless zoom — no loading screens or transitions
  • Single render loop, shared lighting/camera
  • Most immersive "Powers of Ten" feel

Cons

  • Major rewrite — all three views need to coexist in one scene
  • Performance: managing objects across vastly different scales (meters to light-years)
  • Deep Space currently uses SVG, would need full Three.js rewrite
  • Scale management is notoriously hard in 3D (floating point precision)
B

Animated Tab Transitions (Separate Scenes)

Keep tabs as separate scenes but replace instant tab switches with smooth zoom-out/zoom-in animations. Each tab has its own Three.js canvas. Transition: current view fades/zooms out → new view zooms in. Tabs become "zoom level indicators" rather than separate pages.

Pros

  • Incremental — build on existing code for Orbit and Deep Space
  • Each scene optimized independently for its scale
  • No floating-point precision issues
  • Can still feel continuous with good transitions

Cons

  • Not truly seamless — there's a transition moment
  • Slightly less immersive than a single scene
  • Need to coordinate visual continuity (Earth appearance matches between scenes)
